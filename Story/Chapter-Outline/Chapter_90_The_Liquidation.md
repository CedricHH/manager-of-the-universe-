# Outline: Chapter 90 - The Liquidation

**Word Target**: 1,500-2,000 words

---

## Context

**Timeline**: Day 1 of 30-day deadline; Imperial Highway
**Situation**: 30+ coordinated bandits ambush Victor's caravan
**Stakes**: Someone knows his deadline and route (intelligence leak)
**New Discovery**: Bandits = hired mercenaries, not random thieves

---

## Hook (200-300 words)

Victor stands between the bandit leader and Barnaby. The 12 visible bandits think they have the advantage. Then Victor counts the hidden forces in the treeline: 30+ more.

**Key Realization**: "This isn't a toll. This is a contract."

The bandit leader isn't negotiating—he's stalling. Waiting for Victor to reveal more information. His armor is too good. His stance too professional. He's ex-military.

**Inciting Action**: Victor uses `[Performance Review]` on the leader.

```
> [ARMI]
> **Target**: Bandit Leader "Kade"
> **Class**: Sellsword (Level 14)
> **Employer**: [Contract Bound - ENCRYPTED]
> **Mission**: Capture alive. Bounty: 25,000 GP.
```

**Victor's Response**: "Seventy-eight percent probability you were hired by someone who knows I'm worth more alive than dead. Twenty-two percent you're just very stupid. Which is it?"

---

## Development

### Beat 1: The Negotiation (400-500 words)

**Scene**: Victor weaponizes transparency.

**Dialogue**:
- **Kade** (bandit leader): Demands surrender. "We have you surrounded."
- **Victor**: "You have me *valued*. There's a difference. Who hired you?"
- **Kade**: "Confidential. Business is business."
- **Victor**: "Then let's talk business."

**Victor's Offer**:
```
Option A: Fight me. Lose 40-60% of your men. Collect 25,000 GP bounty (minus funeral costs).
Option B: I hire you instead. 30,000 GP. Right now. No deaths.
```

**Kade's Counter**: "How do I know you have that kind of gold?"

**Victor's Move**: Opens the Gilded Caravan's vault door. Stacks of coins glitter inside (visual proof of wealth).

**Complication**: Kade hesitates. He's tempted. But the contract is *magically binding*. If he breaks it, his mana core burns out.

**Contract Clause Revealed**: "Deliver Victor Kaine alive to [REDACTED] by sundown, or forfeit life force."

**Victor's Analysis**: "Suicide clause. Whoever hired you didn't trust you to stay bought. That's sloppy management."

---

### Beat 2: The Loophole (500-600 words)

**Scene**: Victor finds the contract exploit.

**Victor's Proposal**: "I can't buy you out of the contract. But I can buy *the contract itself*."

**Explanation**:
- Every magical contract has a "transfer clause" (standard Consortium template)
- If the original employer dies/forfeits, the contract transfers to the highest bidder
- Victor doesn't need to break the contract—he needs to *become* the contractor

**The Plan**:
1. Kade reveals who hired him (contract allows this for "target verification")
2. Victor uses Galactic Exchange to find employer's vulnerabilities
3. Victor forces employer to transfer contract to him
4. Bandits now work for Victor, contract satisfied

**Kade's Objection**: "You can't just... negotiate your way out of an ambush."

**Victor**: "I've negotiated my way out of board coups, hostile takeovers, and death. This is just Tuesday."

**Key Moment**: Kade checks his contract scroll. The transfer clause IS there. He didn't even know it existed.

**Kade's Decision**: "...Who the hell are you?"

**Victor**: "Someone who reads the fine print."

---

### Beat 3: The Employer Reveal (400-500 words)

**Scene**: Kade reveals who hired him.

**Employer**: **Merchant Vex of the Blackwood Trading Company**

**Victor's Immediate Reaction**: Doesn't recognize the name. Pulls up Alliance network.

**ARMI Search Results**:
```
> **Merchant Vex**
> **Affiliation**: Blackwood Trading Company
> **Specialty**: Cross-border smuggling
> **Territory**: Southern Provinces
> **Intel**: Rumored Church of Eternal Light financier
```

**Victor's Deduction**:
1. Vex isn't tracking Victor randomly
2. Church likely hired Vex as proxy after detecting Galactic Exchange activity
3. Hestia's warning was accurate—mana signatures traced faster than expected
4. This is *pre-emptive* strike (before miracles even hit market)

**Strategic Implication**: "They're not reacting to what I've done. They're reacting to what I *could* do."

**Victor's New Priority**: Eliminate Vex before Church connection solidifies.

**Action**: Victor uses `[Contract Binding]` to force-transfer Kade's contract by threatening to expose Vex's Church ties to rival merchants.

**Leverage**: "If I publish that Vex is a Church proxy, every smuggler from here to Oakhaven will stop doing business with him. His company collapses in 48 hours."

**Result**: Contract transfer successful. Kade and bandits now employed by Victor (Security Division).

---

## Cliffhanger (200-300 words)

**Scene**: Victor hires the 42 "bandits" as caravan security.

**Kade's Question**: "What's our first job, boss?"

**Victor's Answer**: "We're going to visit Merchant Vex. And we're going to make him an offer."

**Barnaby's Reaction** (comedic relief): "Boss, you just got ambushed and you hired the ambushers. Is this normal for you?"

**Victor**: "Extremely normal."

**ARMI Notification**:
```
> **[New Asset Acquired]**
> **Kade's Company**: 42 Sellswords (Average Level 12)
> **Monthly Cost**: 4,200 GP (100 GP per soldier)
> **Classification**: Security Contractor
```

**External Tension**: As the caravan resumes travel, Victor checks the Galactic Exchange.

**Alert**:
```
> **[Listing Status]**
> **Item**: [Miracle: Rain]
> **Offer Received**: 45,000 GP (Full Price)
> **Buyer**: ANONYMOUS
> **Note**: "Urgent delivery requested. Will pay double for immediate transfer."
```

**Victor's Suspicion**: "Someone wants that miracle *very* badly. And they're willing to show their desperation."

**Final Line**: "Barnaby, how fast can this wagon move? We need to reach Oakhaven before someone else realizes what I'm selling."
