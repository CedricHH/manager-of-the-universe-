## Hook (200-300 words)

**Setting**: Core Chamber, Dungeon Insolvia. 6 hours after achieving the Million.

Victor reviews the **[ARMI]** notification that appeared after Consortium contract fulfillment:

> **[ARMI - CONSORTIUM UPDATE]**
> Status: Independent Contractor (Verified)
> Achievement: 1.022M GP in 23 days
> New Tier: **REGIONAL OPERATOR**
> 
> **Next Milestone**: 10,000,000 GP
> Timeline: No deadline (performance-based advancement)
> Benefits Unlocked: [Institutional Analysis], [Market Penetration Protocol]
> 
> **Advisory**: Your success rate places you in top 8% of Restructurers.
> **Recommendation**: Scale operations to match capital base.

**Victor's Analysis**:
- Current assets: 1.022M GP liquid + 4 partnerships + military force
- Dungeon revenue: ~15,000 GP/week (sustainable but insufficient)
- **Problem**: Merchant Guild controls all market licensing (15% fees on transactions)
- **Solution Required**: Bypass Guild licensing via dungeon-based commerce

**Victor's Internal Monologue** (corporate calculation style):
- Dungeon = proven business model, but external sales require Guild permits
- Merchant Guild = 38-year monopoly on trade licensing, headed by Guild Master Lysander Corvus
- **Key Insight**: "I spent Arc 1 *breaking* monopolies. Arc 2 is about *circumventing* them entirely."

**Hook Action**: Sniv enters, carrying intelligence from the city:
- Merchant Guild raised transaction fees to 15% (exploiting post-war recovery demand)
- Public anger rising, but no alternative exists (unlicensed shops get shut down)
- Guild Master Lysander publicly mocked "dungeon merchants" as "barely legal parasites"

**Victor's Response**: "If Lysander wants to charge 15% for the privilege of existing, we'll operate where his charter doesn't apply. The dungeon is Crown territory."

## Development (1,000-1,200 words)

### Beat 1: The Market Analysis (300-400 words)

**Scene**: Victor, Sniv, and Asterion in the Strategy Room (repurposed Core antechamber)

**Victor's Presentation**:
- **Display**: ARMI projects market data onto wall via mana interface
- **Current Merchant Guild Economics**:
  - Market License Fee: 15% of all transactions
  - Shop Rental (Guild-controlled): 50 GP/month (Market Quarter premiums)
  - Non-compliance Fine: 500 GP + shop seizure
  - Market size: 45,000 people, ~10,000 GP/week in Guild fees collected
  - **Total Guild revenue**: ~520,000 GP/year from licensing alone

**Victor's Strategy**:
- Dungeon entrances classified as "Crown Territory" (not subject to City/Guild regulations)
- "Insolvia Apothecary" placed at dungeon entrance = no Guild license required
- Slime-core potions (from owned Potion R&D division) sold direct-to-consumer
- **Price advantage**: No 15% Guild fee = 15% automatic undercut

**Sniv's Reaction**:
- "Boss is making shop... at dungeon entrance? Where adventurers come in?"
- Victor: "Where adventurers come in, yes. And where Lysander's charter doesn't apply."
- Sniv: "This is very clever loophole! Sniv approves of legal circumvention strategy!"

**Asterion's Question** (pragmatic, security-focused):
- "The Merchant Guild will pressure the Crown to close the loophole. They have political connections."
- Victor: "Which is why we're not just selling potions. We're building a political constituency. Every adventurer who saves 15% becomes a voter against Guild regulations."

**Key Reveal**: Victor's goal isn't just to sell potions—it's to create public pressure for Guild reform, weakening Lysander's monopoly across all markets.

### Beat 2: The Production Setup (350-400 words)

**Scene**: Victor tours the Potion R&D Division (formerly Mordecai Groll's operation)

**Marcus Thiel** (alchemist, former Groll employee, now Victor's potion master):
- "Production capacity is 2,000 potions/week using the slime farm output."
- Victor: "Double it. We're opening a storefront."

**Current Asset Review**:
- **Slime Farm Alpha**: 2,000 cores/week, already operational
- **Potion R&D Division**: 15 alchemists (absorbed from Groll's guild)
- **Production Cost**: 3 silver/potion (vs. market price 50 silver)
- **Profit Margin at 10 silver**: 233% (acceptable)

**Victor's Expansion Proposal**:
- Invest 30,000 GP in storefront construction (dungeon entrance, Market Quarter adjacent)
- Invest 15,000 GP in slime farm expansion (double capacity to 4,000 cores/week)
- Projected weekly revenue: 200 GP (first week), scaling to 1,000 GP/week
- ROI timeline: 12 weeks at conservative estimates

**Nova's Input** (via speaking stone):
- "The dungeon entrance expansion will require mana restructuring. I'll need 3% of storefront revenue."

- Nova: "Acceptable."

**ARMI System Integration**:

> New Asset: **Insolvia Apothecary**

### Beat 3: The Trade War Strategy (350-400 words)

**Scene**: Back in Strategy Room. Victor outlines the 4-phase plan.

**Phase 1: Silent Construction** (Weeks 1-2)
- Build storefront at dungeon entrance
- No public announcements
- Train goblin shopkeepers (Sniv's assignment)

**Phase 2: The Launch** (Week 3)
- Open "Insolvia Apothecary" publicly
- Price potions at 10 silver (80% below market)
- Advertise: "Same quality. Fair price. No Guild fees."

**Phase 3: Guild Response** (Weeks 3-5)
- Expected: Political pressure, sabotage attempts, licensing challenges
- Victor's counter: Crown territory status + public support (adventurers love savings)
- Publicity strategy: "Why does the Merchant Guild fear competition?"

**Phase 4: Political Escalation** (Weeks 6-8)
- Guild attempts Crown intervention
- Victor mobilizes adventurer constituency (petition for licensing reform)
- Outcome: Guild forced to reduce fees or lose political support

**Sniv's Assignment**: Recruit 10 goblin "shopkeepers" (teach basic customer service)

**Asterion's Assignment**: Security for apothecary (prevent Guild sabotage)

**Victor's Risk Assessment**:
- Probability of Guild legal challenge: 85%
- Probability of sabotage attempts: 62%
- Probability of Crown intervention: 35% (Crown benefits from dungeon taxes)
- **Acceptable risk threshold**: Exceeded. Proceed anyway.

**Final Line of Beat**: "Sniv, start training goblins. Asterion, hire guards. We open in two weeks."
