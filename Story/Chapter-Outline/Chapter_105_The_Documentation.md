## Development (1,000-1,200 words)

### Beat 1: The Perfect Compliance (300-350 words)

**Scene**: Apothecary operations, mid-morning. Greymark observing.

Victor doesn't avoid Greymark—he invites scrutiny.

**Greymark's Inspection**:
- Health protocols: Perfect (Marcus trained under old Guild standards)
- Storage conditions: Exceeds requirements (dungeon mana-stabilization)
- Product quality: Better than Guild-licensed vendors (controlled slime cultivation)
- Customer service: Exemplary (goblin training = surprising competence)
- Pricing transparency: Clear signage, no hidden fees
- Revenue documentation: Every transaction logged (ARMI audit trail)

**Victor's Tactical Approach**:
- Offers Greymark full access (confidence play)
- Marcus demonstrates potion quality testing (99.7% purity vs. market standard 94%)
- Sniv shows customer satisfaction surveys (improvised—goblins asking "You like potion? Yes/No?")
- Asterion confirms zero security incidents

**Greymark's Internal Frustration** (brief POV):

Twenty years as Chief Inspector. Never seen an operation this clean.

No violations. Not one.

Storage: Perfect. Quality: Exceptional. Service: Professional.

The dungeon merchant isn't cutting corners. He's raising the standard.

How do you shut down a shop that's *better* than Guild regulations require?

**Greymark to Victor**:

"Your operation is... compliant, Mr. Kaine."

"Of course," Victor replies. "I have no interest in health violations. Bad for business."

"The Guild will still need to review your licensing status."

"Crown Territory exemption is quite clear, Inspector. But I welcome the review."

Greymark leaves. No violations to report.

**ARMI Update**:

> **[ARMI - REGULATORY THREAT ASSESSMENT]**

> Lysander's Legal Grounds: NONE (compliance perfect)
> Next Escalation Probability: 89%
> Timeline: 24-48 hours
> 
> **Predicted Response**: Political pressure via City Council

Victor dismisses the interface.

Lysander's Phase 1 failed. Phase 2 incoming.

- **Asterion**: Security for coalition rally (public demonstration planned)

> **Coalition Potential**: 2,500+ adventurers benefit from price reduction

## Cliffhanger (200-300 words)

**Scene**: Market Quarter, late evening. Victor walks through the district.

The apothecary is closed. SOLD OUT sign still hanging.

But the Market Quarter... is changing.

Victor observes:
- Three Guild-licensed apothecaries: CLOSED EARLY (inventory unsold)
- Adventurer groups discussing "the dungeon shop" (positive buzz)
- Merchants whispering about "Guild monopoly finally broken"
- Street vendors selling "I BOUGHT CHEAP POTIONS" commemorative badges (entrepreneurial opportunism)

Public opinion shifting.

ARMI pulses.

> **[ARMI - MARKET INTELLIGENCE ALERT]**
> **SOURCE**: Merchant Guild internal communications (intercepted)
> 
> **URGENT**: City Council hearing ACCELERATED
> Original timeline: 3 days
> New timeline: TOMORROW (emergency session)
> 
> **Motion Filed**: "Unlicensed Commerce Prohibition Act"
> **Sponsors**: Guild-backed Council members (4 of 7)
> **Vote Probability**: 57% passage (if unopposed)
> 
> **Consequence**: Immediate shutdown of all unlicensed shops pending Crown review
> 
> **Timeline**: Hearing at 10 AM

Victor stops walking.

Lysander moved faster than projected.

Tomorrow. Not three days.

He pulls out his speaking stone, channels mana to alert the team.

"Sniv. Asterion. Marcus. Emergency meeting. Core Chamber. Now."

Victor glances back at the apothecary—his clean, compliant, perfectly legal shop that Lysander is trying to destroy through bureaucratic acceleration.

Twelve hours to build a coalition.

Twelve hours to weaponize public opinion.

Twelve hours to prove that cheap potions matter more to voters than Guild revenue.

And somewhere in his office, the BROKEN CROWN data crystal still glows.

Eleven days remaining.

But first: survive tomorrow's Council hearing.

Victor walks toward the dungeon entrance.

Phase 2 begins at dawn.
