## Hook (200-300 words)

**Setting**: Core Chamber, midnight. Emergency strategy session.

Twelve hours to build a political movement that normally took months.

Victor sat at his desk, ARMI projecting tactical data across three dimensional screens. Sniv, Asterion, and Marcus stood ready for assignments.

"Lysander accelerated the timeline to catch us unprepared," Victor said. "He assumes twelve hours isn't enough to organize opposition. We're going to prove him wrong."

**ARMI projection showing**:
- Council Members (7 total): 4 Guild-backed, 2 independent, 1 populist
- Voting threshold: 4 votes needed to pass Prohibition Act
- Current probability (unopposed): 57% passage
- Target: Flip 2 Council members to Victor's side (reduce to 2 Guild votes = failure)

**Strategy Breakdown**:

"We can't win through institutional relationships. Lysander owns those. We win through voter pressure. Make the Council members fear their constituents more than they fear the Guild."

**Assignments** (rapid-fire military precision):

**Sniv**: Customer outreach. Wake every customer from the last two days. Gather testimonials. Target: 500 signed statements by dawn.

**Marcus**: Economic analysis. Calculate how much sales tax the city collected from our 2,000 transactions. Prove our model generates MORE city revenue than Guild fees.

**Asterion**: Adventurer mobilization. Contact every major party in Oakhaven. We need bodies at the Council hearing. Target: 200 adventurers in attendance.

"We have twelve hours," Victor said. "Lysander spent thirty-eight years building political capital. We're going to spend one night showing him that people matter more than institutions."

Team dispersed.

Victor pulled up the BROKEN CROWN data crystal. Ten days remaining on that decision.

But tonight: survive the Council.

**2 AM - Marcus's Economic Analysis**:

The alchemist worked through revenue calculations.

**Calculation**:
- Victor's 2 days: 2,000 potions sold = 20,000 silver revenue
- City sales tax: 5% = 1,000 silver (10 GP) collected
- Guild's model: 200 potions sold (high prices, low volume) = 10,000 silver revenue
- City sales tax from Guild model: 5% = 500 silver (5 GP)

**Conclusion**: Victor's high-volume/low-price model generates DOUBLE the city sales tax vs. Guild's low-volume/high-price model.

**Additional Analysis**:
- Guild fees paid to Guild: 15% of 10,000 silver = 1,500 silver
- City receives: NONE of that (Guild keeps it)
- City loses Guild fees if Victor wins: 0 silver (Guild fees don't go to city anyway)
- City GAINS sales tax if Victor wins: +500 silver per day

Marcus compiled the data into a formal report. Three pages. Charts. Irrefutable math.

### Beat 3: The Council Hearing (400-450 words)

**Scene**: Council Hall, 10 AM.

The chamber was packed.

Seven Council members at the high table. Lysander and Victor at opposing podiums. Three hundred adventurers filling every seat and standing along walls.

Guild guards at doorways. Tension thick enough to cut.

**Council Chairman Aldus** (neutral, elderly, 30 years service) called the session to order.

"Motion: Unlicensed Commerce Prohibition Act. Sponsor: Guild Master Lysander Corvus. We will hear arguments, then vote."

**Lysander's Opening** (3 minutes):

"Honored Council. For thirty-eight years, the Merchant Guild has maintained order in Oakhaven's markets. Our licensing system ensures quality, protects consumers, and funds eighteen percent of the city's annual budget through Guild fees.

Mr. Kaine's operation bypasses this system. While technically legal under Crown Territory law, it sets a dangerous precedent. If every dungeon claims exemption, our entire regulatory framework collapses. Guild revenue—and city funding—disappears.

I propose the Unlicensed Commerce Prohibition Act: All commercial operations within city limits must obtain Guild licensing, regardless of Crown Territory status. This protects our economy and preserves municipal funding."

Polite applause from Guild-backed Council members.

**Victor's Response** (3 minutes):

"Councilors. Mr. Corvus argues I'm stealing Guild revenue. But let's examine reality.

**First**: Legal compliance. My operation operates under the Dungeon Commerce Act of 1147—foundational trade law affecting every dungeon in the kingdom. Revoking that requires Crown Council approval, which could take years and impact thousands of businesses.

**Second**: Economic benefit. In two days, I sold two thousand potions. The city collected ten gold pieces in sales tax. Under the Guild's model —high prices, low volume—the city would collect five gold pieces. My model generates double the municipal tax revenue.

**Third**: Voter impact."

Victor gestured to the packed chamber.

"Three hundred adventurers are in this room. They represent twelve percent of Oakhaven's voting population. They save forty silver per potion at my shop—real money that stays in their pockets, gets spent in this city, and grows our economy.

The Guild controls prices because they can. I offer competition. That's not theft. That's free market economics.

Your constituents are watching. Choose wisely."

The chamber erupted. Adventurers cheering. Guild members scowling.

**Council Deliberation**:

Chairman Aldus called for discussion.

**Councilor Brod** (Guild-backed): "The Guild system has worked for decades!"

**Councilor Vex** (populist): "But citizens are paying five times market value! Where's the consumer protection?"

**Councilor Dren** (independent, up for re-election): *glances nervously at crowd of voters*

Victor locked eyes with Dren. The councilor looked away.

Pressure working.

## Cliffhanger (200-300 words)

**The Vote**:

Chairman Aldus called for the vote.

"All in favor of the Unlicensed Commerce Prohibition Act?"

Four hands raised (Guild-backed members). Expected.

"All opposed?"

Two hands: Councilor Vex (populist). Councilor Dren (independent).

Victor's lobbying worked. Two flipped.

Wait.

That's 4-2. One abstention.

Victor counted again. Seven Council members total. Four votes YES, two votes NO.

That left one undecided: **Councilor Mira Sterling** (minor nobility, swing vote).

She hadn't voted yet.

Chairman Aldus: "Councilor Sterling?"

The chamber fell silent.

Mira Sterling—mid-40s, practical administrator, neutral political stance—looked at Lysander. Then at Victor. Then at the three hundred adventurers watching.

She raised her hand.

OPPOSED.

**Final Vote**: 4-3. Motion FAILS (requires 5 of 7 to pass legislation).

The chamber exploded. Adventurers cheering. Guild members stunned.

Victor remained still, expression neutral. Calculated victory.

Lysander's face was stone. Thirty-eight years of political capital. Defeated by twelve hours of populist organizing.

**But Lysander wasn't done.**

As the Council adjourned, he approached Victor directly for the first time.

"Impressive, Mr. Kaine," Lysander said quietly, voice cold. "You won today. But this isn't over. The Guild has... other resources."

"Is that a threat?"

"It's a professional courtesy. Warning you that Phase 3 begins tomorrow."

Lysander walked away.

ARMI pulsed urgently.

> **[ARMI - THREAT DETECTION]**
> Lysander Corvus: Escalation probability 94%
> Phase 3 prediction: UNKNOWN (creative solutions promised)
> Timeline: 24-48 hours
> 
> **WARNING**: Lysander has exhausted legal and political options
> **Next move**: Extra-legal or indirect pressure likely
> **Recommendation**: Prepare for unconventional threats

Victor dismissed the interface.

He'd won the Council vote. But Lysander's warning was clear.

Phase 3 coming. Unknown methods. Dangerous.

And somewhere in his office, the BROKEN CROWN crystal pulsed.

Nine days remaining.

Victor left the Council Hall, mind already calculating next moves.

Because Lysander still had thirty-eight years of connections.

And desperate bureaucrats were often the most dangerous.
