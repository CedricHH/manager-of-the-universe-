## Development

### Beat 1: Guild Internal Conflict (300-400 words)

**Parallel Scene—Guild Hall, 10 AM** (2 hours before meeting):

- **Guild Council Emergency Session**: Hard-liner faction (Council Member Thane + 6 Guild vendors) demand Lysander reject Victor's terms
- Thane: "Thirty-eight years maintaining Guild independence. You're selling out to a dungeoneer who's been in Oakhaven for two weeks!"
- **Lysander's Response**: Professional, data-driven
  - Shows economic projections: Continued resistance = 40% revenue loss within 2 weeks
  - Guild member bankruptcies begin Day 14
  - City Council charter revocation by Week 6
  - Alternative: Integration preserves Guild structure, protects member pensions, reduces fees (benefits all sides)
- **Hard-liner Counter-Proposal**: "Launch pressure campaign. Deny services to adventurers buying from Insolvia. Force Victor's customers to choose."
- **Lysander's Calculation**: Pressure campaign = short-term disruption, long-term PR disaster (customers resent coercion, Guild loses goodwill)
- **Decision**: Lysander overrules hard-liners. "We negotiate. If terms are unacceptable, we walk away. But we listen first."
- **Vote**: 4-3 (narrow victory for negotiation approach)

### Beat 2: The Negotiation (800-900 word)

**Noon—Private Meeting Chamber, Guild Hall**:

- **Attendees**: Victor + Valerius (advisor) vs Lysander + Chief Inspector Greymark (witness)
- **Opening**: Professional, respectful (two competent operators assessing merger terms)

**Victor's Baseline Proposal** (restates written offer):
1. Guild fees: 15% → 5% (18-month phased reduction)
2. Charter restructuring: Reduce bureaucratic overhead, streamline licensing
3. Lysander = Director of Regulatory Compliance (advisory role, reports to Victor)
4. Transition: 36 months
5. Member protection: No forced departures, pension obligations honored

**Lysander's Concerns** (professional negotiation):
- **Concern 1**: Fee reduction timeline too aggressive (members need revenue transition support)
  - **Victor's Counter**: Extend to 24 months (slower phase-out), but commit to 5% final rate
  - **Lysander**: Acceptable with written guarantee
- **Concern 2**: "Advisory role" = neutered authority (perception of defeat)
  - **Victor's Counter**: Lysander retains operational authority over compliance/licensing enforcement. Victor owns strategic direction; Lysander executes implementation. "I need someone who understands Guild members. You built these relationships."
  - **Lysander**: Requests title change: "Vice Director of Commerce" (dignified, operational authority clear)
  - **Victor**: Acceptable
- **Concern 3**: Member protection vague (what about vendors directly competing with Victor?)
  - **Victor's Counter**: Direct competitors (potion vendors) offered: (A) Employment in Insolvia operations at 150% current income, or (B) Transition fund (6 months salary) to establish non-competing businesses
  - **Lysander**: Requests 12-month transition fund (longer safety net)
  - **Victor's Calculation**: 12 months = 18,000 GP additional cost, but prevents resentment/resistance. Acceptable.

**Critical Moment**: 
- Lysander: "This isn't a negotiation. It's a controlled dissolution with a dignity clause."
- Victor: "It's a restructuring. Guild infrastructure has value. Your institutional knowledge has value. I'm buying that value, not destroying it."
- **The Question**: "Why not just wait six weeks and buy the assets at liquidation prices?"
- **Victor's Answer**: "Because liquidation creates unemployment, destroys institutional knowledge, and generates resentment. Integration preserves what works, replaces what doesn't. More efficient outcome."

**Lysander's Recognition**: Victor isn't motivated by dominance or revenge. He's optimizing systems. The Guild isn't personal—it's just inefficient infrastructure that can be improved.

### Beat 3: Agreement Reached (300-400 words)

**Modified Terms (Final Agreement)**:
1. Guild fees: 15% → 5% (24-month phased reduction—extended from 18)
2. Charter restructuring: Joint committee (Victor + Lysander + 3 Guild representatives)
3. Lysander = **Vice Director of Oakhaven Commerce Authority** (operational authority, reports to Victor strategically)
4. Transition: 36 months (unchanged)
5. Member protection:
   - No

 forced departures
   - Direct competitors: Employment offer (150% income) OR 12-month transition fund
   - Pension obligations honored (unchanged)
6. **NEW**: Guild Hall renamed "Oakhaven Commerce Authority Headquarters" (preserves institutional identity)

**Total Cost to Victor**: ~95,000 GP (increased from original 87,000 due to extended transition fund)

**Lysander's Acceptance**: Professional handshake. "We have an agreement. I'll present to Guild Council this afternoon."

**Victor's Assessment**: 
- 95,000 GP to prevent 6-week disruption, preserve institutional knowledge, maintain market stability
- ROI: Positive (avoids customer loss during transition chaos, retains Lysander's expertise)

**Final Exchange**:
- Lysander: "You could have crushed us. Why didn't you?"
- Victor: "Crushing generates inefficiency. Integration generates value. I prefer the latter."
