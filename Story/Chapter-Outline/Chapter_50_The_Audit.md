# Chapter 50: The Audit [WRITTEN]

## Chapter Goal
Victor executes his first counter-move in the Regulatory Warfare protocol. Convert the Guild surveillance from a threat into an opportunity by inviting the scouts into the dungeon for an "official audit" — on Victor's terms. End with a cliffhanger that escalates the Guild conflict and closes Act 1.2.

- **Lead Guild Scout** (new): A by-the-book investigator who expected a monster den, not a boardroom.

- **Valerius**: Legal/Compliance. Terrified but useful.

## Plot Beats

### 1. The Hook (200-300 words)
- Dawn. Victor stands at the dungeon entrance with a tea tray.
- The scouts think they're hidden. They're not.
- Victor waves at the treeline. "Would you like some tea? You've been out there since yesterday. That can't be comfortable."
- The scouts freeze. Their cover is blown.

### 2. The Invitation (300-400 words)
- Victor walks toward the treeline with theatrical calm
- Identifies the lead scout (Guild badge visible through Victor's ARMI analysis)
- Offers a formal invitation: If they're investigating, why not do it properly?
- "You're welcome to audit us. Openly. With full access. I insist."
- The scouts are suspicious but can't refuse — a formal audit is their mandate
- ARMI: **[Social Engineering: Weaponized Transparency]**

### 3. The Tour (400-500 words)
- Victor gives the lead scout (and two assistants) the "executive tour"
- Sniv presents immaculate paperwork: entry logs, revenue records, safety protocols
- Asterion demonstrates the "Non-Lethal Containment" policy (shows the goblins have training manuals)
- The dungeon looks... legitimate. Professional. *Boring*.
- The scouts are confused. Where are the bodies? The torture chambers? The monster hordes?
- "This is a licensed recreational facility," Victor explains. "The monsters are employees."
- ARMI: **[Audit Status: Hostile Inspector → Confused Inspector]**

### 4. The Revelation (300-400 words)
- Lead scout finds something: The Core Chamber
- Nova is visible but described as "automated infrastructure" — not a sentient partner
- Scout is suspicious but can't prove anything
- Victor offers complete transparency: "Ask anything. Check any room. I have nothing to hide."
- This is the trap. Complete openness makes them look for hidden threats — and find none.
- BUT: The lead scout finds ONE irregularity: "Your permit... is provisional. Granted by Lord Sterling."
- Victoria smiles thinly. "Is that a problem?"
- Scout: "It will be. Lord Sterling's territorial authority doesn't supersede Guild jurisdiction."

### 5. The Cliffhanger (200-300 words)
- The scouts leave. They found nothing illegal — but they found leverage.
- Victor's permits are under Sterling's authority. The Guild can challenge that authority.
- Valerius panics: "They're going to invalidate our permits! They'll petition the Crown!"
- Victor watches the scouts disappear into the forest. Calculating.
- "Then we need the Crown on our side before they do."
- Nova pulses. "THE CROWN? THAT IS... A SIGNIFICANT ESCALATION."
- Victor: "The Guild thinks they're playing politics. They forget: I *invented* this game."
- He turns to Sniv. "Get me everything we have on Crown Prince Aldric. His debts. His vices. His ambitions."
- Sniv: "We go... UP?"
- Victor: "We go all the way up."
- ARMI: **[Strategic Escalation: Act 1.2 Complete. Act 1.3: Guild Pressure — INITIATED]**
