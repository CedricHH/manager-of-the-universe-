- Intelligence Officer, political network specialist
- **Personality**: Rapid-fire analysis, pattern recognition obsession
- **Opening assessment**: "I've reviewed your Oakhaven operations. Eighty-seven distinct pressure points exploited across three institutions in sixteen weeks. Guild Master Lysander—thirty-eight-year veteran—capitulated via economic siege, not violence. Efficiency profile matches Tier 4 predictive models."
-**Victor's reaction**: First time being analyzed **by** someone at his competence level

**Resh's warning**: "Previous Restructurers failed because they treated war like economics. Solvaris doesn't negotiate. It kills."

### Beat 2: Terra-Solvaris Intelligence Briefing (400-500 words)

**Hologram activates**: Full planetary display. Terra-Solvaris orbital view.

**Kira presents data**:

**Planet specs**:
- Population: 2.4 billion
- Tech level: Late industrial (firearms, artillery, early mechanization)
- Mana density: 340% above Terra-Insolvia (magic more powerful, more volatile)
- Government: COLLAPSED (5-way civil war, 18 months active conflict)

**5 Factions** (color-coded territories):

1. **Legitimists (Blue) - 15% territory**
   - Claim: Royal lineage (Princess Celestia, age 19, last surviving royal)
   - Strength: Legal claim, popular support in rural areas
   - Weakness: No military, no capital, survived via defensive fortifications
   - Capital: Thornhaven (northern mountains, 340k population)

2. **Revolutionaries (Red) - 25% territory**
   - Claim: Democratic mandate ("People's Council")
   - Strength: Largest faction, popular urban support, ideological unity
   - Leadership: Tribune Cassian Vex (former general, charismatic)
   - Weakness: Fractured command structure, 12 subfactions
   - Capital: New Valeris (industrial center, 890k population)

3. **Theocrats (White) - 20% territory**
   - Claim: Divine mandate (Church of the Sacred Flame)
   - Strength: Magical superiority (Tier 3-4 mages), zealot infantry
   - Leadership: Pontifex Mathias Kael (political operator, not true believer)
   - Weakness: Resource-poor territory, no industrial base
   - Capital: Sanctum Aurea (temple city, 670k population)

4. **Merchant Coalition (Gold) - 30% territory**
   - Claim: Economic necessity ("Stability through commerce")
   - Strength: Controls 60% agriculture, 75% trade routes, wealthiest faction
   - Leadership: Executive Council (7 merchant lords, consensus rule)
   - Weakness: No ideology, purely transactional, members will betray for profit
   - Capital: Portus Aurum (trade hub, 1.2M population)

5. **Warlord Confederation (Black) - 10% territory + Central Bank**
   - Claim: "Might is right" (military control)
   - Strength: **Controls Central Bank** (16.7M GP), best-equipped military
   - Leadership: **General Kael Dravik** (former Royal Guard Commander, tactical genius)
   - Weakness: Smallest territory, hated by all other factions, unsustainable long-term
   - Capital: Ironhold Fortress (military installation, 180k population)

**Critical intel**:
- **Central Bank location**: Formerly Solvara (old capital, now no-man's-land between 3 factions)
- **Consortium investment**: 16.7M GP in vaults, Dravik controls access
- **Civilian casualties**: 340,000 dead (18 months), 1.2M displaced
- **Escalation timeline**: Revolutionaries preparing offensive (Est. 6 weeks), Theocrats stockpiling mana weapons, Merchant Coalition negotiating with all sides

**Victor's question**: "What's Consortium's preferred outcome?"

**Kira's answer**: "Functional government capable of repaying debt. We don't care which faction wins. Legitimist monarchy, Revolutionary democracy, Theocratic regime, Merchant oligarchy—irrelevant. Stability + capital recovery = success."

**Mira's addendum**: "Optimal strategy: Back Merchant Coalition. They're transactional, economically motivated, already control majority infrastructure. Install them, recover capital, stabilize via economic dependency."

**Resh's counter**: "Merchants have no military. Dravik won't negotiate. Theocrats won't accept secular rule. Revolutionaries will execute merchants as class enemies. 'Optimal' assumes rationality. War doesn't work that way."

**Victor's analysis begins**: Five factions. Zero trust. Everyone fighting everyone. Question isn't who to back—it's how to make backing irrelevant.

### Beat 3: Mission Parameters & Departure Prep (350-450 words)

**Kira presents mission clock**:

- **4-month timeline** (Consortium Board mandate)
- **Success metrics**: 
  - Functional government (single authority, >60% territorial control)
  - Capital recovery (13.36M GP minimum = 80% threshold)
  - Civilian casualty minimization (Board PR concern—bad optics if >500k total deaths)

**Failure triggers Terminal Resolution**:
- Month 4 expires without government: Orbital strike authorized
- Capital recovery <50%: Mission deemed unrecoverable
- Team casualties >50%: Survivors extracted, planet liquidated
- Victor's death clause: Activates independently of team survival

**Resources provided**:
- 500,000 GP emergency authorization (Capital Requisition already granted)
- Consortium diplomatic credentials (limited immunity, faction-dependent recognition)
- ARMI upgraded: Real-time intelligence integration, faction movement tracking
- Dimensional extraction beacon (emergency evac, 72-hour cooldown)

**Equipment**:
- Victor: Consortium formal attire (political meetings), ARMI interface crystal, encrypted comm device
- Resh: Tier 4 combat loadout (enchanted blades, barrier generator, tactical armor)
- Mira: Intelligence kit (scrying focus, encryption suite, 47 false identities)

**Insertion plan**:
- **Landing zone**: Neutral territory (ruins of old capital Solvara, near Central Bank)
- **Cover story**: "Independent arbitrators" (Consortium backing undisclosed initially)
- **First contact**: Merchant Coalition (Mira's recommendation—transactional, least likely to shoot first)
- **Timeline**: Insert today, establish base, initiate contact within 48 hours

**Resh's final warning**: "Oakhaven had contracts. Solvaris has bullets. First time someone points a gun at you, remember: You're not bulletproof. I am. Stay behind me."

**Victor's response**: "Noted. But bullets are expensive. I'll make shooting me unprofitable."

**Resh's grin**: "Maybe you will survive."

## Cliffhanger (250-350 words)

**1100 hours**. Briefing complete. Dimensional gate to Solvaris opens in 30 minutes.

**Victor final check**: ARMI link to Oakhaven. Live feed: Public announcement happening NOW (noon Oakhaven time, noon Platform local time synchronized).

**Live feed**: Guild Hall plaza. Lysander addressing crowd (via ARMI visual):

> "Forty years I've led this Guild. Today marks transition, not ending. Fees reduced: fifteen percent to five. Commerce Authority activated. Oakhaven prospers."

Crowd cheers. Adventurers celebrating. Marcus visible in background (Acting CEO, confident posture). Sniv coordinating (18 goblins managing crowd). Asterion on perimeter (security overwatch).

**Oakhaven: Stable. Autonomous. Thriving.**

Victor closes feed.

**Kira's observation**: "You built something that doesn't need you. Rare for Restructurers. Most hoard control."

**Victor's reply**: "Control is liability. Infrastructure is asset. Oakhaven will outlive me. That's the point."

**Dimensional gate activates**: Reality tears open. Terra-Solvaris coordinates locked.

**Mira hands Victor dossier**: "Merchant Coalition Executive Council profiles. Memorize during transit."

**Resh checks blade enchantments**: "Transit takes four minutes. I'll secure landing zone first. Mira second. You last. Stay close."

**Kira's final instruction**: "Four months. Thirteen-point-three-six million gold minimum. Two-point-four billion lives. Don't waste them."

**Victor steps toward gate**. Looks back at Platform Sigma—Consortium infrastructure, cosmic operations, hundreds of agents.

**Thought**: "They think I'm their tool. They built Oakhaven as my foundation. Now Solvaris will be my weapon. The long game continues."

**Final line**: Victor Kaine walked through the dimensional gate.

Oakhaven behind him. Solvaris ahead. Eighty-four hours since signing death contract.

War waiting.
