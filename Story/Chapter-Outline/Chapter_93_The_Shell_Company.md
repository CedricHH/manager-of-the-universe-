**Word Target**: 1,500-2,000 words

---

## Context

**Timeline**: Day 2 of 30-day deadline; approaching Oakhaven
**Previous**: Coalition needs 70 miracles urgently; Church hunting suppliers
**Stakes**: Scale arbitrage operation while avoiding Church detection
**Capital**: 130,930 GP

## Hook (200-300 words)

Victor analyzes the scaling problem inside the moving caravan:

**The Math**:
- Coalition demand: 70 miracles
- Current weekly purchase limit: 25,000 GP (Tier 0 reputation)
- Miracle cost average: 40,000 GP each
- **Capacity**: 0.625 miracles per week
- **Timeline to fulfill**: 112 weeks (over 2 years)

**Bottleneck**: Galactic Exchange reputation system gates purchase limits.

**ARMI Analysis**:
```
> **Reputation Tiers**:
> Tier 0 (current): 25k GP/week limit
> Tier 1 (10 transactions): 100k GP/week + Shell Company Access
> Tier 2 (50 transactions): 500k GP/week + Bulk Vendor Access  
> Tier 3 (200 transactions): UNLIMITED + G.A.C. Direct Line
```

**Current Progress**: 3/10 transactions to Tier 1

**Victor's Realization**: He doesn't need to buy 70 miracles. He needs to **unlock Tier 1** (shell companies) to multiply purchase limits.

**Strategy**: Each shell company = separate account = separate 25k GP weekly limit.

**Math**:
- 10 shell companies × 25k GP/week = 250k GP buying power
- 250k GP ÷ 40k GP per miracle = 6.25 miracles/week
- 70 miracles ÷ 6.25 = **11.2 weeks** (fits within 30-day deadline if he completes remaining 7 transactions FAST)

**Problem**: How to execute 7 transactions in 2 days?

**Inciting Action**: Victor opens Exchange and searches: "High-volume, low-cost consumables."

### Beat 3: The Shell Network (400-500 words)

**Scene**: Victor registers 10 shell companies to multiply buying power.

**Shell Company Registration Interface**:
```
> **Cost**: 5,000 GP per shell
> **Benefit**: Each shell = independent Tier 0 account (25k GP/week)
> **Limit**: 50 shells maximum (requires Tier 2 for unlimited)
> **Legal Status**: Technically compliant (Consortium allows asset diversification)
```

**Victor's Investment**: 10 shells × 5,000 GP = 50,000 GP

**New Buying Power**:
- Main Account (Tier 1): 100,000 GP/week
- 10 Shells (Tier 0 each): 250,000 GP/week
- **Total**: 350,000 GP/week

**Miracle Production Capacity**:
- 350,000 ÷ 40,000 GP per miracle = **8.75 miracles/week**
- Coalition needs 70 miracles
- Fulfillment timeline: **8 weeks** (well within 30-day deadline if he prioritizes Coalition orders)

**Victor's Strategic Plan**:
1. Week 1-2: Deliver 17 miracles to Coalition (2 estates priority)
2. Week 3-4: Remaining 53 miracles (rolling deliveries)
3. Maintain Consortium deadline focus (other arbitrage for 1M GP goal)

**Capital Status After Shell Registration**:
- Starting: 130,930 GP
- Spent on shells: -50,000 GP
- Profits from micro-trades: +35,000 GP
- **Current Balance**: 115,930 GP

**Kade** (confused): "Boss, I don't understand half of what you just did."

**Victor**: "I turned money into infrastructure. Infrastructure into capacity. Capacity into leverage."

**Kade**: "...So we're rich?"

**Victor**: "We're **scaled**."

## Cliffhanger (200-300 words)

**Scene**: Victor receives priority message from Hestia (G.A.C. sponsor).

**Galactic Exchange Alert**:
```
> [PRIORITY MESSAGE - HESTIA_AUDITOR]
> Subject: Tier 1 Achievement Flagged
> Status: URGENT
```

**Message**:
> "Victor. Congratulations on Tier 1. But you triggered Compliance Review. Ten transactions in 48 hours + shell company registration = algorithmic red flag for money laundering. G.A.C. Audit Division dispatched. ETA: 72 hours. They will review ALL accounts. If they find Church-related arbitrage, your entire operation freezes. Suggest: sanitize records, prepare explanations, or... pay the expedited clearance fee (25,000 GP). Your move. -H"

**Victor's Analysis**:
- G.A.C. Audit = existence-level threat (they can terminate Consortium contract)
- Church already suspicious
- 72 hours = arrives before Coalition delivery timeline
- Fee: 25,000 GP (acceptable loss to avoid shutdown)

**ARMI Calculation**:
```
> **Option A**: Pay fee (safe, expensive)
> **Option B**: Risk audit (free, potentially catastrophic)
> **Option C**: Negotiate audit terms (uncertain, time-intensive)
```
