## Development

### Beat 1: Emergency Assessment (400-450 words)

**Strategy Session**: Victor convenes team (Marcus, Asterion, Sniv) for crisis analysis.

- **Marcus Reports Scope**: Contacted all backup suppliers—every major operation either refuses service or "mysteriously" has stock shortages
- **Lysander's Strategy Revealed**: Guild Master leveraged 38 years of institutional relationships to create coordinated blacklist
- **ARMI Economic Analysis**: 
  - Day 4: Apothecary closes (zero inventory)
  - Day 7: Customer exodus begins (82% market share loss)
  - Day 14: Operation bankrupted (Guild monopoly restored)
- **Victor's Assessment**: "He's using social capital as a weapon. Thirty-eight years of handshakes, favors owed, institutional loyalty. This is how established power fights disruption."
- **BROKEN CROWN Pressure**: 8 days remaining on decision deadline—cannot afford week-long supply crisis

**Victor identifies two parallel solutions**:
1. **Immediate**: External procurement (non-Guild supplier, 2-day travel minimum)
2. **Long-term**: Vertical integration (dungeon-based reagent synthesis)

### Beat 2: The Countermeasures (500-600 words)

**Immediate Response (External Procurement)**:

- **Marcus + Asterion Mission**: Travel to **Westhold** (neutral merchant city, 2-day journey each way)
- Westhold has independent alchemical suppliers (non-Guild affiliated, higher prices but available)
- Mission objective: Secure 7-day emergency supply (buys time for long-term solution)
- Budget allocated: 2,500 GP (3x normal cost, but necessary crisis expense)
- Departure: Immediately (Marcus knows contacts, Asterion provides security)
- **Timeline**: 4-day round trip (returns Day 4 = just before shutdown)

**Long-Term Solution (Vertical Integration)**:

- **Victor + Nova Strategy**: Reverse-engineer reagent production using dungeon resources
- Concept: Slime cores already substitute for basic mana storage—can dungeon biomes produce reagent-equivalent materials?
- **Nova's Analysis**: Floor 6 (planned Deep Forest biome) could generate herb analogues
- **Alternative**: Existing floors contain mineral/organic compounds that might substitute
- **Victor's Approach**: Start experimental synthesis immediately while Marcus secures emergency supply
- **Assigns Sniv**: Coordinate with Nova on Floor 1-5 resource surveys (identify potential reagent substitutes)

**Lysander's Blind Spot**:
- Victor recognizes Lysander's assumption: Victor is purely a merchant (buy low, sell high)
- Lysander doesn't understand Victor controls **production infrastructure** (the dungeon itself)
- Guild Master thinks in terms of supply chains; Victor thinks in terms of **asset creation**
- This will be Lysander's strategic error

### Beat 3: The Investigation (350-450 words)

**Parallel Action**: While countermeasures deploy, Victor investigates Lysander's network.

- **ARMI Social Mapping**: Visualizes Merchant Guild relationship network (38 years of connections)
- Discovers: Lysander personally mentored 12 of 15 major suppliers; attended weddings, funerals, business launches
- **Institutional Power Demonstrated**: This isn't bribery or coercion—it's genuine loyalty earned over decades
- **Victor's Realization**: "I can't replicate this. Institutional trust takes time I don't have. But I don't need to match his network—I need to make it irrelevant."

**Kira Appears (Brief Scene)**:

- Does not intervene (Liaison evaluates, doesn't assist)

**Marcus/Asterion Departure**:
