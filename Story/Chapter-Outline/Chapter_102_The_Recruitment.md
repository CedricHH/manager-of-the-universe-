## Hook (200-300 words)

**Setting**: Market Quarter, Oakhaven. Early morning. 3 days after Chapter 101.

Victor enters the Market Quarter disguised (hooded cloak, mana signature suppressed). He's hunting the mystery Restructurer signal.

**ARMI Interface**:
> **[ARMI - MARKET INTELLIGENCE]**
> Quest: [Market Intelligence]
> Objective: Identify unknown Restructurer-class mana source
> 
> **Tracking Protocol**: ACTIVE
> Signal Strength: 34% (intermittent)
> Last Detection: Market Quarter, Alchemist Supply Shop
> Distance: 200 meters

Victor follows the trail to a small potion shop. The mana signature pulses from inside.

**Inside the shop**: A young woman (mid-20s) examines potion ingredients. She's dressed as a traveling merchant, but her movements are too precise. Too calculated.

She picks up a slime core sample. Inspects it. Sets it down.

Then she turns and looks directly at Victor through the crowd.

Smiles.

The mana signature vanishes—suppressed, just like his.

She walks out the opposite exit.

**Victor's Assessment**:
- She knew he was tracking her
- She wanted him to find her
- She's baiting him into contact

**Decision**: Not today. Victor has operations to oversee. The mystery Restructurer can wait.

**Transition**: He returns to the dungeon to check on apothecary preparations.

## Development (1,000-1,200 words)

### Beat 1: The Production Assignment (350-400 words)

**Scene**: Potion R&D Division (formerly Groll's workshop, now Victor's asset), Dungeon Floor 3.

**Context**: Victor absorbed Mordecai Groll's Alchemist Guild in Arc 1 (Chapters 78-79). Groll is now "Head of Potion R&D" under Victor. The 15 alchemists from the Guild work for Victor.

**Marcus Thiel** (age 34, senior alchemist, 12 years experience): Victor's choice to lead the apothecary potion production.

**Victor's Assignment**:
- "Marcus, you're being promoted. You'll run the apothecary operation."
- Salary: 150 GP/month (triple the old Guild rate under Groll)
- Benefits: Housing, meal stipend, profit-sharing (1% of apothecary revenue)
- Work conditions: 6-day work week, no Guild-era exploitation

**Marcus's Reaction**: "The new storefront? The one bypassing Merchant Guild licensing?"

**Victor**: "Crown territory exemption. Dungeon entrance. Lysander Corvus doesn't get a cut."

**Marcus's Hesitation**: "Lysander has political connections. He'll pressure the Crown to close the loophole."

**Victor**: "Which is why we're creating a constituency of adventurers and common folk who benefit from our prices. When Lysander moves against us, he moves against everyone who saves money shopping here."

**Marcus realizes**: Victor isn't just selling potions—he's building political leverage.

**ARMI Update**:
> **[ARMI]**
> Personnel Assignment: Marcus Thiel (Alchemist, Level 8)
> Role: Apothecary Operations Lead
> Salary: 150 GP/month
> Production capacity: 40 potions/day (solo), 120/day (with assistants)

**Victor's Note**: "You'll have two assistant alchemists from the R&D pool. Pick your team."

### Beat 3: The Security Audit (350-450 words)

**Scene**: Market Quarter construction site. Future location of Insolvia Apothecary (dungeon entrance).

Asterion tours the building with Victor. The minotaur has hired 12 additional guards (mix of ex-military, dungeon veterans).

**Security Assessment**:
- **Perimeter**: Stone walls, reinforced doors, anti-scrying wards
- **Internal**: Panic room for inventory, emergency escape tunnels to dungeon
- **Personnel**: 3 guards per 8-hour shift (24-hour coverage)
- **Rules of Engagement**: Non-lethal force unless attacked with lethal intent

**Asterion's Concern**: "The Merchant Guild will send pressure. Maybe not enforcers—they're not the violent type like the old Alchemist Guild was. But inspectors. Bureaucrats. Legal harassment."

**Victor**: "Which is why the storefront is on dungeon property. Crown Territory. City regulations don't apply, and neither does Lysander's guild charter."

**Asterion**: "What if they target our supply chain? Interfere with deliveries?"

**Victor**: "Our supply chain runs through the dungeon. Slime farm is internal. Marcus's production is internal. The only external touchpoint is the customer-facing storefront."

**Asterion's Second Concern**: "What about the mystery mana signature you detected?"

Victor pauses.

**Victor**: "I saw her this morning. Young woman, mid-twenties. Another Restructurer."

**Asterion**: "Enemy?"

**Victor**: "Unknown. She's watching me. I'm watching her. We're both waiting to see who makes the first move."

**Asterion**: "And if she moves?"

**Victor**: "Then we'll find out if the Consortium trains us to compete or cooperate."

**ARMI Notification** (interrupts conversation):

> **[ARMI - CONSTRUCTION UPDATE]**
> Asset: Automated Slime Farm Alpha
> Status: 87% complete
> First harvest: 2 days
> Projected output: 2,000 cores/week (on target)

**Victor**: "Two days until we have product. One week until operations are ready. Two weeks until we open."

**Asterion**: "And then?"

**Victor**: "Then Lysander finds out what happens when someone tries to tax a Restructurer."

## Cliffhanger (200-300 words)

**Scene**: Victor returns to Core Chamber. Late evening.

He's reviewing the construction updates when ARMI pulses an unexpected message:

> **[ARMI - PRIORITY MESSAGE]**
> Source: UNKNOWN RESTRUCTURER
> Encryption: Consortium Standard Protocol
> 
> **Message Received**:
> "To: Restructurer #REDACTED (Victor Kaine)
> From: Restructurer #418 (Designation: Liaison)
> 
> **Subject**: Territorial Introduction
> 
> You're not subtle. Neither am I. 
> 
> Meet me at the Broken Barrel Inn, tomorrow, noon.
> Come alone. Bring your ARMI interface.
> 
> I'm not here to compete. I'm here to *evaluate*.
> 
> - Kira"

Victor stares at the message.

**Analysis**:
- She knows his Restructurer ID (Consortium-level intelligence access)
- She's designation "Liaison" (not combat, not acquisition—*oversight*)
- She's evaluating him (for what? promotion? termination?)
- She signed her name (confidence, or trap?)

**Probability Matrix**:
- Trap: 23%
- Genuine Consortium oversight: 54%
- Recruitment attempt: 18%
- Unknown third option: 5%

**Victor's Options**:
1. Attend meeting (risky, but information-rich)
2. Ignore (safe, but loses intelligence opportunity)
3. Send proxy (cowardly, reveals weakness)

He dismisses options 2 and 3 immediately.

**Decision**: He'll attend. Alone. Armed with information and leverage.

**Final Line**: 

Victor replies via ARMI:

> "Accepted. Noon. Alone. 
> Fair warning: I don't take orders well.
> - V.K."

He sends the message.

Tomorrow, he'll meet the Restructurer who's been shadowing him.

Tonight, he'll prepare for every possible outcome.

**Final ARMI Note**:

> Status: PROGRESSING
> New Objective: Survive meeting with Restructurer #418
