## Development (1,000-1,200 words)

### Beat 1: The Acceleration Decision (300-350 words)

**Scene**: Strategy Room. Victor, Sniv, Asterion, Marcus.

**Victor's Presentation**:

"Original timeline: Two weeks construction, then launch. New timeline: We open tomorrow."

**Asterion's Question**: "Why the change?"

"Kira mentioned the Merchant Guild has Crown connections. If Lysander moves first, he can petition for a territorial ruling. If we launch first, we establish precedent. First mover advantage."

**Sniv's Excitement**: "Goblins are ready! Customer service training complete! Synergy is MAXIMUM!"

**Marcus's Concern**: "We only have one day's inventory. Two thousand cores = two thousand potions. What if we sell out in hours?"

**Victor's Analysis**:

"We will sell out. That's the point. Scarcity creates demand. Lysander taxes sellers fifty percent more with fees and charges premium prices because he can keep inventory in stock. We charge eighty percent less and run out immediately. Which one looks more valuable?"

**Marcus realizes**: Artificial scarcity as marketing.

**Victor continues**:

"Phase two production starts tonight. The farm produces two thousand cores per week. We'll have continuous supply. But day one? We make noise. We force Lysander to respond before he's coordinated."

### Beat 3: The Launch / Lysander's Response (350-400 words)

**Scene**: Guild Master's Tower, dawn. Lysander receives Victor's letter.

*[Note: This is from Lysander's POV - rare perspective shift for dramatic effect]*

Guild Master Lysander Corvus was a man who appreciated order.

Thirty-eight years running the Merchant Guild. Thirty-eight years of controlled licensing, predictable fees, and compliant merchants.

The letter arrived with his morning tea.

He read it once. Twice. Three times.

**Lysander's Internal Reaction**:

"Crown Territory. This... dungeon merchant... is claiming Crown Territory exemption.

If he sells without a license and succeeds, every dungeon in the kingdom could do the same.

This isn't competition. This is precedent."

Lysander set down the letter carefully.

He activated his communication crystal. "Summon Chief Inspector Greymark. Now."

Within the hour, his key advisor arrived:
- Chief Inspector Aldwin Greymark (enforcement lead, 20 years experience)

**Lysander's Opening**:

"A dungeon merchant is selling potions at eighty percent below market prices. From Crown Territory. Without a license."

**Greymark's response**: "Technically legal. The Dungeon Commerce Act grants Crown exemption to dungeon-based operations."

"Then we need to close the loophole."

**Greymark's Assessment**: "That requires Crown Council approval. Could take months."

**Lysander**: "What can we do now?"

**Greymark**: "Surveillance. Document his operations. Find violations. If he's not following health protocols, we have grounds."

**Lysander**: "Then go. Watch him. Find something."

**Greymark**: "And if there are no violations?"

**Lysander**: "Then we'll have to be... creative."

## Cliffhanger (200-300 words)

**Scene**: Insolvia Apothecary, dawn. Grand opening.

Victor stands outside the shop as the sun rises over Market Quarter.

The line already stretches down the block.

Word spread fast: Health potions for ten silver. Eighty percent cheaper than market.

Adventurers. Merchants. Common laborers who couldn't afford market prices. All waiting.

**Sniv bounces excitedly**: "Boss! Boss! SO MANY CUSTOMERS! This is VERY good for quarterly revenue!"

Asterion surveys the crowd. "This is crowd control nightmare. If they rush the door..."

"They won't," Victor says. "Sniv, open the doors. Four customers at a time. Keep the line orderly."

The doors open.

Within two hours, 800 potions sold. Revenue: 8,000 silver (80 GP).

Within four hours, 1,600 potions sold. Revenue: 16,000 silver (160 GP).

**At hour five**, the shelves are nearly empty.

And then Chief Inspector Greymark arrives.

Two Guild inspectors. Formal robes. Carrying clipboards.

They don't threaten. They just... observe. Take notes. Ask pointed questions.

**Greymark to Victor**: "Interesting operation, Mr. Kaine. Crown Territory exemption is... novel."

"Entirely legal," Victor responds.

"We'll see." Greymark smiles. It doesn't reach his eyes. "We'll be watching."

They leave.

**Final ARMI Notification**:

> Guild Inspector surveillance confirmed

> **Recommendation**: Prepare legal defenses

Victor closes the shop at noon. SOLD OUT.

Phase One complete: Market disruption successful.

Phase Two begins tomorrow: Survive Lysander's legal counter-attack.

And somewhere in the background, the BROKEN CROWN data crystal still glows on his desk.

Thirteen days to decide.
