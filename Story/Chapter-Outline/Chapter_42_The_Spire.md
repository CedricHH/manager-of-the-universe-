# Outline: Chapter 42 - The Spire

**Word Target**: 1500-2000

## Hook
Victor arrives at the Spire—the headquarters of the Alchemist Guild. It is a monolith of black stone and glass, designed to make visitors feel small. Kael and Mira accompany him to the gate, but the guards block them.
"The Guildmaster invited Mr. Kaine. Alone."
Kael protests. Victor dismisses them. "Wait in the lobby. If I'm not down in 90 minutes, invoice the Guild for my life insurance policy."

## Development
### Beat 1: The Architecture of Power
Victor ascends via a magical lift. He analyzes the building's layout. It's efficient, defensible, and expensive.
Private dining room at the top. Floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking the city.
Guildmaster Groll is waiting. He is not fat or soft. He is elderly, scarred, and looks more like a retired general than a potion brewer.
The table is set for two.

### Beat 2: The Audit
Dinner is served. No poison—Groll considers that beneath him.
Conversation is a duel. Groll lists Victor's recent moves: the price war, the fire, the contracts.
"You move like a soldier," Groll says. "But you think like a banker."
Groll reveals his own philosophy: Alchemy is order. The Cartel keeps the world stable. Victor is chaos.
Victor counters: "You aren't order. You're stagnation. Stagnation is death."

### Beat 3: The Buyout
Groll makes his offer. It's generous.
"Join us. We will give you a seat on the Council. We will adopt your production methods. You will be rich."
The condition: Victor must hand over his "source" (Zip/The Dungeon).
Victor declines. "I don't want a seat. I want the table."

### Beat 4: The Threat
Groll's demeanor changes. The temperature drops.
He reveals he hasn't just been watching the market. He's been watching the *forest*.
"We know you didn't just 'find' those potions, Mr. Kaine. We know about the Dungeon."
Blackmail. "Yield, or we expose you as a Dungeon Lord to the King."

## Cliffhanger
Victor stops eating. The threat is genuine. Being a Dungeon Lord is a capital offense in the city.
Victor smiles. "You think that's leverage?"
"It's a death sentence," Groll says.
"No," Victor says. "It's a hostile work environment."
He stands up. "Negotiation failed. Initiation of hostile takeover protocols confirmed."
He leaves. Groll watches him go, deciding whether to kill him now or later.
Ending: Victor exits the Spire. He looks at Kael. "We have a problem. The timeline just accelerated."
