## Development (1,000-1,200 words)

### Beat 3: The Offer

Victor's voice echoes through the corridor (via Core's speaker system):

> "Adventurers. I'm offering you a deal. Pay the entry fee, navigate the dungeon, keep 100% of loot. No Guild cut. No registration. No questions."

Mira: "Who are you?"

Victor: "The Manager."

### Beat 4: The Negotiation

Gareth wants to fight. Mira wants information.

Victor sweetens the deal:
- Floor 1-3: Standard challenge, fair loot
- Floor 4: Optional boss fight (Asterion), premium rewards
- Death insurance: 50 GP refund to next of kin if killed

Mira: "You're... monetizing the dungeon?"

Victor: "I'm optimizing it. Your choice: pay and play, or leave."

### Beat 5: The Split

Gareth: "We don't negotiate with monsters."

Mira: "We don't usually encounter monsters with accounting degrees either."

Tessa (young rogue): "I vote we pay. This is... interesting."

Bron: Silent. Shrugs.

**Decision**: 3-1. They pay 20 GP entry fee.

## FLASHBACK: Discovery of the Truth (Continued)

### The Investigation

Victor couldn't stop looking at the photos.

He was a corporate shark. He'd laid off thousands. But children?

He began digging. Used old contacts. Called in favors.

The evidence multiplied:
- Board meeting minutes (they knew)
- Supplier contracts (Victor's signature, but above his clearance)
- Shipping manifests (conflict zone origins hidden)

### The Realization

Victor's infamous "efficiency protocols" hadn't just optimized supply chains.

They'd **accelerated demand** for materials that came from mines where children died.

He'd never asked. That was the crime.

Not active evil. Passive blindness. The spreadsheet didn't have a column for "human cost."

## Cliffhanger (200-300 words)

### Present: Floor 2

The Iron Wolves advance through the Fungal Gardens.

Sniv guides them. Points out "optional challenge areas" (with extra loot for extra risk).

Mira: "This is... actually well-designed."

Gareth: "It's a trap."

Victor (via speaker): "Of course it's a trap. All dungeons are traps. The question is: whose trap benefits you more? Mine, or the Guild's 20% cut?"

### The Counter-Offer

Mira makes an offer:

> "What if we become... repeat customers? Discount rate?"

Victor pauses.

This is new. Adventurers asking for a subscription model.

> "10% discount for return visits. 15% if you bring referrals."

Gareth: "This is insane."

Tessa: "This is brilliant."

**Profit so far**: 20 GP entry fee
**Target**: 22.5 GP (50% increase)
**Current Status**: Already at 133% of baseline. And they haven't reached Floor 3 yet.
