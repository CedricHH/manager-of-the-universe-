## Backstory

### The True Believer

Elara was a model Paladin—devoted, competent, principled. She rose through the Church of Eternal Light through pure merit, earning the rank of Knight-Commander under High Priestess Aurelia.

She believed in the system. She enforced the rules. She recruited others to the faith.

### The Discovery

Elara discovered corruption within the Church: High Priestess Aurelia was embezzling tithes meant for orphanages.

She had proof:
- Financial documents
- Witness testimony
- Years of systematic theft

She reported it through proper channels.

### The Betrayal

Aurelia buried the evidence.

Elara was framed for heresy.

Stripped of her rank, her powers, her title.

Blacklisted across three kingdoms.

Her family estate—sold to pay "legal fees" from the Church's prosecution.

Total assets liquidated: **6,400 GP**

### The Rebuilding

Elara took loans from the Merchant's Guild (1,600 GP at 8% APR) to supplement her capital.

**Total available: 8,000 GP**

She invested everything—her entire net worth—into the Rivercrest franchise partnership with Victor Kaine.

## Psychology

### Core Traits

- **Shattered Faith**: Still believes in justice. No longer believes institutions serve it.
- **Moral Clarity**: Won't compromise her principles, but pragmatic about methods.
- **Competent**: Former Knight-Commander didn't rise on luck. She's strategic, thorough, disciplined.
- **Vengeful (Controlled)**: Aurelia destroyed her. The Church destroyed her. She'll rebuild, then settle accounts.

### Philosophical Position

Elara sees Victor clearly: He's exploitation dressed in contracts.

But he's **honest** about it.

The Church pretended to be holy while robbing the faithful. Victor makes no pretense.

In a twisted way, Victor's transparent exploitation is more ethical than the Church's hypocrisy.

She hates that this is true.

She hates that she's choosing to work with him.

But she has 8,000 gold pieces and no other options.

---

## Relationship with Victor

**Mutual Respect (Wary)**

Victor recognizes Elara's pattern: Betrayed by her institution. Just like him (the board, Project Cobalt).

He doesn't exploit this. Instead, he **uses her morality as a management tool**:

- Accepts her ethical clauses (-18% profit margin)
- Because they reduce regulatory risk (-65% Church intervention probability)
- And ensure Elara enforces standards she believes in

Elara recognizes this manipulation.

She accepts it anyway.

Because Victor's terms are better than any alternative, and the franchise gives her:
1. Purpose
2. Independence
3. A platform to rebuild

### The Irony

Elara is partnering with a dungeon operator to maintain ethical standards the Church never upheld.
