## Origin Story

### The Golden Child

Elara was everything a paladin should be: faithful, brave, compassionate. Born to a minor noble family, she rose through the Church of Eternal Light on pure merit. By 25, she was "The Sword of Dawn"—the youngest paladin to earn the title.

She believed. Truly believed. In the Light. In justice. In the system.

### The Church's True Nature

The Church of Eternal Light was a pyramid scheme.

Faithful "invested" prayers and gold. The returns went only to top clergy. Miracles were reserved for major donors. The poor received platitudes.

Elara didn't know. She was the public face—the genuine article used to legitimize the fraud.

### The Fall

When Elara's father fell ill, she requested a healing miracle. She had given the Church ten years of devoted service. Surely they would help.

They refused. Her family's "investment level" was insufficient.

In desperation, her parents took loans from Church-affiliated lenders. The interest was 40%. Compounding monthly.

Her mother died from stress. Her father followed, broken. The Church seized their estate for "unpaid debts."

Elara demanded answers. The High Priest explained, calmly, that faith was about "spiritual returns, not material ones."

She attacked him.

She didn't land a single blow. The Temple Guards were ready. They had done this before.

### Excommunication

Elara was stripped of her title, her powers, and her armor. The official charge: "Breach of Sacred Contract, Section 14: Insubordination."

The Church didn't kill her. That would create a martyr.

Instead, they sold her debt to collectors. 

When Victor bought that debt, Elara owed 847,000 gold pieces—more than most kingdoms' treasuries.

## Psychology

### Core Traits

- **Shattered Faith**: She still believes in justice. She no longer believes any institution serves it.
- **Righteous Anger**: Burns constantly beneath the surface. Controlled. Barely.
- **Self-Loathing**: She trusted the Church. She recruited others. How many did she damn?
- **Trapped**: Victor's contract is ironclad. She cannot attack him. She cannot leave. She cannot speak against him.

### The Irony

Elara hates Victor. But Victor is honest about being a bastard.

The Church pretended to be holy while robbing the faithful. Victor makes no pretense. His exploitation is transparent, detailed in contracts everyone can read.

In a twisted way, Victor is more ethical than her former masters.

She hates that she sees this.

## The Binding (Predatory Lending)

Victor used [Predatory Lending] when Elara needed mana to save orphans from a fire.

**The Contract**:
- Victor "loaned" her 500 mana points
- Interest: 20% per day
- Collateral: Her life force

**Current Status**:
- She can never repay the compounding debt
- If she leaves Victor's party: instant coma
- If she attacks Victor: contract breach → death
- If she speaks against Victor: [NDA] activates → mouth voxelizes, censorship beep

She is, legally and magically, property.
